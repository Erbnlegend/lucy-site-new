Lucy Erb

I am an installation based sculpture artist exploring themes of cross cultural symbolism and the psychology of how we perceive beauty through nature. To accomplish this I create sculptures using metal, wood, fiber, and when possible, local sustainable materials. I hope to create an inspirational experience that people of all ages and backgrounds can enjoy. I believe that we are all alike in mind when searching for beauty and mysteries of our world.


The techniques I enjoy most are mixed media watercolor, carving, silk painting, and fine metal working. I also have some experience with felting, weaving, screen printing, woodturning, welding, vacuum forming and many other techniques. I am always looking for new ways to expand my artistic knowledge and am willing to try any form of creative expression.

Bio

Lucy Erb grew up in Gilbert, Arizona. In may 2016 she received a BFA degree in Sculpture from Arizona State University, Herberger Institute for Design and the Arts. During her years at ASU Lucy studied woodworking, metal fabrication, fiber techniques, and 2D media. She also learned innovative technologies at Techshop Candler including CNC cutting and waterjet. As part of the ASU experience Lucy has been a member of Metals Club where she attended glass casting workshops, held leadership positions and participated in the semi annual metal art sale.

In the spring of 2016 she was the Co-president of The Sculpture Collective and had a major part in planning the biannual end of the semester sculpture show, Blueshift. Lucy has exhibited her art in exhibitions in Tempe and Phoenix. Lucy Erb has a permanent commissioned piece in the ASU Health Services Wellness Collection and plans to do more public art pieces in coming years.
